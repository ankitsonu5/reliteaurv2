Everyday Gluten Free: Seamless Swaps for Traditional Indian Recipes

For many, wheat is woven into the very fibres of family meals—golden chapatis puffing on iron griddles, tart samosas on festival trays. Gluten, though, doesn’t play nice with everyone. The trick with gluten free living? Reinvent—not replace—what you love. Use jowar for rotis with earthy complexity; replace maida in cakes with hearty oat flour. Rice flour transforms into chewy, satisfying dosa and crisp pakora batters. Chickpea flour, with all its nutty bravado, elevates both savoury snacks and breakfast pancakes. Myths vs. Facts: What Everyone Should Know About Gluten Free Diets

“Gluten free is just a fad.” “It’ll fix everything.” “It’s only for the sick.” These are myths. Let’s clear the plate: If you’re celiac, gluten free is non-negotiable. For others, it’s an option—sometimes smart, sometimes unnecessary. Gluten free products aren’t automatically healthier; some pack in extra starch and sugar, chasing texture and shelf life. That’s why reading labels matters—fibre, protein, real ingredients win every day. The best gluten free foods are those that focus on real nourishment, not just absence. Whole foods like millets, potatoes, and legumes are gluten free by nature and full of nutrients. If you aren’t medically required to steer clear of gluten, check with your doctor before overhauling your diet. But if curiosity or taste guides you, lean into flavour and freshness, not fads. The truth? In the kitchen, what matters most isn’t what’s missing—it’s what’s added, with care and craft. Myths begone. Eating Out Without Gluten: Tips for Safe, Delicious Restaurant Experiences

The hardest part of gluten free living isn't avoiding the obvious—it’s spotting the sneaky wheat in sauces, thickeners, and grill baskets. Dining out in India, where menus read like food festivals and hospitality means endless refills, can be perilous for celiacs. The key? Advocate for yourself—don't settle for vague assurances. Ask about cross-contamination, ingredient sourcing, and preparation spaces. But don’t lose hope—awareness is up, and savvy restaurants now offer labelled gluten free options. Some chefs even love flexing their creativity, whipping up gluten free naans or millet-based cakes. Carry a translation card if needed; it makes dietary needs clearer. Remember, your experience shapes the future for others: every time you speak up, every positive review shared, you’re not just feeding yourself—you’re nurturing a culture that values safe, tasty, inclusive dining.
